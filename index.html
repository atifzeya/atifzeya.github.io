By day I navigate the quiet currents of markets as a Quant Risk Analyst at UBS, translating pulses of data into the language of resilience. I wear the hat of a pseudo-economist too — part skeptic, part dreamer — always sketching patterns where others see only noise. Numbers are my compass; curiosity, my true north.

Beyond screens and models I am an avid traveler, gathering fragments of sky, language, and light like souvenirs. My free hours are stitched from pages and pixels: I lose myself in books that open new worlds, and in photo edits that coax memories into sharper focus. Each image is a small pilgrimage; each sentence, a quiet expedition.

I am from Calcutta, and the city is a constant map on my skin. Whenever I return, I wander its lanes on long walks, listening to the hum of history beneath the pavement and tracing stories worn into walls and riverbanks. In those footsteps I find the past and present braided together — an intimate geography that keeps calling me home.
